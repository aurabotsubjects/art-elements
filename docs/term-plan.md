# Art Explorer — Term Plan (Year 5–6)
### Elements of Art & Principles of Design | 10 Weeks | 5 Lessons/Week | 40 Minutes/Lesson

---

## 1. Purpose & Vision

Art Explorer is a visual, interactive app that walks Year 5–6 students through the **7 Elements of Art** and **6 Principles of Design**, one manageable bite at a time, over a full 10-week term. Every lesson is short (40 min), highly visual, and built around *doing* — looking, touching, experimenting, and playing with a concept until it's familiar — rather than passively reading about it.

Each day in the app has:
- A **student view**: visual intro, interactive exploration activity, and a hands-on task.
- A **"Lesson Plan" button** (teacher-only) that pops up a printable/on-screen lesson plan following the same template as the school's existing plans (see Section 5).

By the end of the term, students should be able to **name, recognise, and use** every element and principle confidently, and apply several of them together in a final synthesis project.

---

## 2. NZC Alignment (The Arts – Visual Arts, Level 3)

| Strand | Achievement Objective |
|---|---|
| **Developing Practical Knowledge** | Explore, select, and use art-making conventions (line, shape, colour, texture, etc.) purposefully. |
| **Developing Ideas** | Develop and revisit visual ideas in response to a variety of stimuli, and use the elements and principles to communicate them. |
| **Communicating and Interpreting** | Describe and interpret visual language, symbols, and artworks from their own and others' work, including NZ/Māori contexts where relevant. |

**Key Competencies woven through every lesson:**
- *Managing Self* — persisting through open-ended exploration, managing materials/time.
- *Relating to Others* — sharing ideas, giving/receiving peer feedback during gallery walks.
- *Thinking* — experimenting, reflecting, making creative decisions and justifying them.
- *Using Language, Symbols & Texts* — using correct art vocabulary to describe their own and others' work.

## 3. Term Overview (10 Weeks at a Glance)

| Week | Focus | Concepts Introduced |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Elements of Art — Intro & Line** | What is "Art Language"? / Line |
| 2 | **Shape & Form** | Shape / Form |
| 3 | **Colour** | Hue, Value, Intensity |
| 4 | **Value & Space** | Value (contrast) / Positive & Negative Space |
| 5 | **Texture + Elements Synthesis** | Texture / Combine all 7 Elements |
| 6 | **Principles of Design — Intro & Balance** | What is "Composition"? / Balance |
| 7 | **Emphasis & Movement/Rhythm** | Emphasis / Movement & Rhythm |
| 8 | **Pattern** | Pattern (deep dive, 2 activity styles) |
| 9 | **Proportion/Scale & Unity/Variety** | Proportion & Scale / Unity & Variety |
| 10 | **Final Synthesis Project** | Elements + Principles combined — "Design a World" project & class exhibition |

**Weekly rhythm (used in every week, adjusted for content):**

| Day | Purpose |
|---|---|
| **Monday** | **Discover** — visual hook, short interactive intro, vocabulary, "spot it in the world" activity |
| **Tuesday** | **Experiment A** — guided hands-on/digital exploration of the concept, low-stakes, playful |
| **Wednesday** | **Experiment B** — a second, different way of exploring the same concept (variety of media/technique) |
| **Thursday** | **Create** — mini artwork applying the concept with more student choice |
| **Friday** | **Reflect & Share** — gallery walk, peer feedback, vocabulary check, artwork goes into digital portfolio |

This 5-day rhythm gives each concept genuine time to sink in (repetition, multiple modes, low pressure) while still producing a finished piece and a reflection moment every single week.

## 4. Week-by-Week Lesson Map

### Week 1 — Elements of Art: Introduction & Line
**WALT:** We are learning to recognise that art is made of building blocks (the Elements of Art), and to use line with purpose and control.

| Day | Lesson Title | Activity Snapshot |
|---|---|---|
| Mon | *What Makes Art, Art?* | Interactive "spot the 7 elements" slider over famous/NZ artworks; introduce the 7 Elements as a visual poster/word wall |
| Tue | *Lines Have Feelings* | Digital line-drawing tool: students draw "angry," "calm," "bouncy," "sleepy" lines; compare as a class |
| Wed | *Line Hunt* | Photo/drawing exploration: find and sketch lines in the classroom/playground (contour, implied, zigzag, curved) |
| Thu | *My Line Story* | Create a small artwork using only line (ink pen/digital) to tell a mood or story — no shapes allowed |
| Fri | *Gallery Walk: Line* | Share artworks, use vocabulary (contour, gesture, implied line) to describe a partner's work; save to portfolio |

### Week 2 — Shape & Form
**WALT:** We are learning to tell the difference between 2D shape and 3D form, and use both intentionally.

| Day | Lesson Title | Activity Snapshot |
|---|---|---|
| Mon | *Flat vs. Solid* | Visual sort: geometric vs organic shapes; shape vs form comparison (circle vs sphere, square vs cube) |
| Tue | *Shape Builder* | Interactive drag-and-drop: build a creature/building from geometric + organic shapes |
| Wed | *Form From Shading* | Explore how shading turns a flat circle into a sphere (light source experiment, digital slider tool) |
| Thu | *Shape City* | Create a skyline/creature/pattern artwork combining geometric and organic shapes with intention |
| Fri | *Gallery Walk: Shape & Form* | Peer share; identify shape vs form in each other's work |

### Week 3 — Colour (Hue, Value, Intensity)
**WALT:** We are learning how colour is built from hue, value, and intensity, and how colour choices create mood.

| Day | Lesson Title | Activity Snapshot |
|---|---|---|
| Mon | *Colour Detective* | Interactive colour wheel; explore hue families, warm vs cool |
| Tue | *Mixing Lab* | Digital paint-mixing tool: mix hues, then lighten (tint) and darken (shade) — see value change live |
| Wed | *Turn Up the Volume* | Intensity slider tool: dull a colour down / make it pop; match colours to moods |
| Thu | *Colour Mood Painting* | Paint/digital artwork expressing a chosen emotion using only colour choices |
| Fri | *Gallery Walk: Colour* | Guess the mood of a partner's painting from colour alone; discuss as a class |

### Week 4 — Value & Space
**WALT:** We are learning to use value to create depth, and to understand positive and negative space.

| Day | Lesson Title | Activity Snapshot |
|---|---|---|
| Mon | *Light to Dark* | Interactive value scale (1–10 greyscale); spot value in black-and-white photos |
| Tue | *Shadow Play* | Torch + object shadow exploration (physical) photographed/drawn to show value ranges |
| Wed | *Positive & Negative* | Cut-paper silhouette activity: explore figure/ground reversal digitally and physically |
| Thu | *Depth Illusion Artwork* | Create a piece using value shading + positive/negative space to suggest 3D depth |
| Fri | *Gallery Walk: Value & Space* | Identify where value creates depth and where negative space is used in peers' work |

### Week 5 — Texture + Elements Synthesis
**WALT:** We are learning to create and represent texture, and to identify all 7 Elements of Art working together.

| Day | Lesson Title | Activity Snapshot |
|---|---|---|
| Mon | *Touch and See* | Sensory table: real textures (bark, fabric, sand, feathers) vs. visual/implied texture in images |
| Tue | *Rubbing & Marking* | Texture rubbing activity (crayon rubbings) + digital texture brush exploration |
| Wed | *Fake It* | Create *implied* texture using only line, shading, and pattern (no real texture materials) |
| Thu | *7 Elements Treasure Hunt Artwork* | Create one artwork that deliberately includes all 7 elements; self-checklist as they go |
| Fri | *Elements Showcase & Quiz* | Gallery walk + fun interactive quiz/game reviewing all 7 Elements before moving to Principles |

### Week 6 — Principles of Design: Introduction & Balance
**WALT:** We are learning that Principles of Design are the "rules" for arranging elements, starting with balance.

| Day | Lesson Title | Activity Snapshot |
|---|---|---|
| Mon | *From Ingredients to Recipe* | Intro to the 6 Principles as "rules" for using elements; visual comparison of balanced vs unbalanced compositions |
| Tue | *Symmetry Mirror* | Interactive symmetrical balance tool — build one half, mirror it automatically |
| Wed | *Balancing Act* | Asymmetrical & radial balance exploration with movable shape pieces (digital collage) |
| Thu | *Balanced Composition Artwork* | Create an artwork demonstrating a chosen type of balance |
| Fri | *Gallery Walk: Balance* | Peers identify symmetrical/asymmetrical/radial balance in each other's work |

### Week 7 — Emphasis & Movement/Rhythm
**WALT:** We are learning to create a focal point (emphasis) and guide the eye using movement and rhythm.

| Day | Lesson Title | Activity Snapshot |
|---|---|---|
| Mon | *Find the Star* | Visual search: spot the focal point in famous artworks; discuss how the artist drew the eye there |
| Tue | *Spotlight Tool* | Interactive activity: use colour/contrast/size to create emphasis on a plain scene |
| Wed | *Eye Journey* | Explore movement/rhythm using repeated shapes/lines that lead the eye across a composition |
| Thu | *Focal Point Artwork* | Create an artwork with one clear focal point and a sense of visual movement |
| Fri | *Gallery Walk: Emphasis & Movement* | Peers trace with their finger/cursor where their eye travels in a partner's work |

### Week 8 — Pattern (Deep Dive)
**WALT:** We are learning to design and repeat pattern with control, including patterns inspired by nature and culture.

| Day | Lesson Title | Activity Snapshot |
|---|---|---|
| Mon | *Pattern Spotting* | Visual hunt for pattern in nature, buildings, fabric, NZ/Māori kōwhaiwhai and tukutuku designs |
| Tue | *Build a Repeat* | Interactive digital tile tool: design one motif, watch it tile/repeat automatically |
| Wed | *Pattern Remix* | Hands-on stamping/printmaking exploration: create a physical repeating pattern |
| Thu | *Pattern Artwork* | Create a finished pattern-based artwork (digital or physical) with a clear repeat unit |
| Fri | *Gallery Walk: Pattern* | Share and identify the repeat unit in each other's patterns |

### Week 9 — Proportion/Scale & Unity/Variety
**WALT:** We are learning to use proportion and scale for effect, and to balance unity with variety in a composition.

| Day | Lesson Title | Activity Snapshot |
|---|---|---|
| Mon | *Big & Small* | Visual comparison of realistic vs exaggerated scale in art (e.g., giant flowers, tiny people) |
| Tue | *Scale Playground* | Interactive resize tool: change proportions of objects in a scene and discuss the effect |
| Wed | *Same but Different* | Unity vs variety sorting activity; explore a composition that's "too samey" vs "too chaotic" and fix it |
| Thu | *Proportion & Unity Artwork* | Create an artwork playing with unusual scale while keeping overall unity |
| Fri | *Gallery Walk: Proportion & Unity/Variety* | Peers discuss what's exaggerated and what ties the piece together |

### Week 10 — Final Synthesis: "Design a World" Project & Exhibition
**WALT:** We are learning to combine multiple Elements and Principles intentionally in one finished artwork, and to talk about our choices like an artist.

| Day | Lesson Title | Activity Snapshot |
|---|---|---|
| Mon | *Plan Your World* | Recap all 13 concepts via interactive review game; students choose 3+ elements & 2+ principles to focus on; sketch planning |
| Tue | *Build — Session 1* | Studio time: begin final artwork (digital or physical), teacher confers 1:1 using planning sheet |
| Wed | *Build — Session 2* | Continue final artwork; peer check-ins using a simple element/principle checklist |
| Thu | *Finish & Write* | Complete artwork; write/record a short artist statement naming the elements/principles used and why |
| Fri | *Class Exhibition* | Whole-class gallery walk / exhibition (physical display and/or app portfolio showcase); celebrate and reflect on the term |

## 6. App Structure (Student-Facing)

- **Home / Term Map**: visual path (like a game map) showing Weeks 1–10, unlocking as the term progresses; each week shows its 5 days as stepping stones.
- **Daily Lesson Screen**: big visual hook image/animation → short "watch/look" moment → interactive exploration tool (drag, draw, slider, sort, mix) → "create" canvas or physical-task instructions → reflection prompt.
- **My Portfolio**: every Friday artwork (and any Thursday piece) auto-saves here, building a visual record of the whole term.
- **Word Wall**: a running, student-friendly glossary of the 7 Elements + 6 Principles, illustrated with examples pulled from their own class's artworks over the term.
- **Teacher Mode**: toggle to reveal the **Lesson Plan pop-up button** on every day, plus a simple progress dashboard (which days each student/class has completed).

## 7. Design Principles for the Build

1. **Visual-first**: every concept is shown before it's explained in words; minimal text on student screens.
2. **Play before product**: Tuesday/Wednesday exploration activities are low-stakes and undoable — no "wrong" answers, encouraging genuine experimentation.
3. **Repetition with variety**: each concept is met at least 4 times (see, do, do differently, make) before the Friday share, without feeling repetitive because the *mode* changes each day.
4. **Consistent rhythm**: the Mon–Fri structure never changes, so students (and the teacher) always know what kind of lesson is coming, reducing cognitive load.
5. **Culturally responsive**: where natural (e.g. pattern/kōwhaiwhai in Week 8), links to Aotearoa/Māori art traditions are included, consistent with Communicating & Interpreting AOs.
6. **Teacher control, student flow**: teachers get the full lesson plan on demand; students get a clean, distraction-free, game-like flow.
